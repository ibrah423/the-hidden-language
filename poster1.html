Creation and Distribution

The slogan appeared on posters, banners, and newspaper headlines during the Anschluss (annexation of Austria) and later during the occupation of other territories. Copies were hung in schools, government buildings, and along parade routes for mass rallies. Because the posters were printed and distributed by state agencies, they could be reproduced on a large scale and placed in highly visible locations. Today, surviving originals are preserved in archives such as the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum, which has collected and digitized examples from confiscated Nazi records.
