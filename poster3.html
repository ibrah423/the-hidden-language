Background & Purpose

This propaganda poster was created to promote the 1933 Law for the Prevention of Hereditarily Diseased Offspring. Under this policy, individuals labeled as ‚Äúgenetically unfit‚Äù including those with disabilities, epilepsy, mental illness, or chronic poverty were subject to compulsory sterilization. The poster reframed this coercive policy as a humane, almost charitable intervention.

Where It Was Displayed

  • Hospitals and public health clinics
  • Genetic counseling centers
  • School and university racial hygiene exhibitions
  • Government-sponsored medical education lectures

Visual & Psychological Strategy

The poster uses calm posture, soft color tones, and clinical imagery to mask the violence of forced sterilization. This gentle aesthetic creates trust in medical authority and frames sterilization as a comforting solution, rather than a violation of fundamental human rights.

Historical Importance

Propaganda like this normalized the idea that some lives were burdens to society. Once sterilization appeared compassionate, the public was more easily led to accept more extreme policies including the T4 euthanasia program, which murdered tens of thousands of disabled Germans. This poster marks a key transition from discriminatory policy to biological violence.
