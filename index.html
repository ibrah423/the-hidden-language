Introduction

This project explores how the Nazi regime used mass media especially posters to shape public thinking, normalize discrimination, and embed racial ideology into everyday life. Propaganda in Nazi Germany was not simply political messaging; it was a coordinated psychological system designed to alter beliefs, emotions, and social behaviors.

The Reich Ministry of Public Enlightenment and Propaganda

Led by Joseph Goebbels, this ministry centralized control over:

  • Newspapers and journalism
  • Film, art, literature, and theater
  • Radio broadcasts and public speeches
  • Posters, exhibitions, and scientific-looking ‚Äúracial education‚Äù materials

By saturating public life with approved information, the ministry created a controlled environment where Nazi ideology appeared natural, scientific, and widely supported.
