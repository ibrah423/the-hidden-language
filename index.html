Introduction

This project explores how Nazi propaganda used imagery, strategic framing, and controlled media to shape public attitudes and normalize discrimination. Posters, speeches, newspapers, and broadcasts worked together to create a worldview that justified exclusion and violence as necessary for national survival.

The Propaganda Ministry

Led by Joseph Goebbels, the Ministry of Public Enlightenment and Propaganda oversaw nearly every cultural or informational channel in Germany. Media alignment ensured that ideology was not simply taught but immersed into daily life.

  • Centralized control of news, art, radio, and education
  • Promoted Hitler as the embodiment of national unity
  • Defined Jews and other minorities as existential threats
  • Used repetition and emotional imagery to guide public belief
